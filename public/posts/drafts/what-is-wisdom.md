Decisions have effects that we can only guess at, so we can only view these effects probabalisticly. Each decision can have multiple effects. The tricky part is that those effects can in themselves also cause successive effects which makes the final outcome of your decision even more hidden.

Each effect can do three things:
1. Good
2. Neutral
3. Bad

Thus, we can assign each decision what we may determine to summararily be overall a  probablistically good, neutral, or bad decision.

Let me explain. I'm using the term good here without a moral conotation. So this could be something that you perceive to be good.

A neutral effect would be 

Mature Christians consistently make wise decisions; the most mature Christians make the wisest decisions.

Wisdom is accurately evaluating decisions when you don't what their effects will be and choosing the one that has the highest chance of having the best outcome based on the past experiences of others or yourself. It's only limited to your information-space. While also taking into account the information that you lack.

So, strictly speaking, you can make the wisest decision that ends up with the worst outcome.
