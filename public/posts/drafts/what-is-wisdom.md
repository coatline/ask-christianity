# How is Wisdom Applied?

Decisions have effects that we can only guess at, so we can only view these effects probabalisticly. Each decision can have multiple effects. The tricky part is that those effects can in themselves also cause successive effects which makes the final outcome of your decision even more hidden.

Each effect can do three things:
1. Good
2. Neutral
3. Bad

Thus, we can assign each decision what we may determine to summararily be overall a  probablistically good, neutral, or bad decision.

In such a case, we can likely say that shooting a gun with no training is unwise.
Unless, of course, there is a high enough probability that something good may come from shooting the gun and a low enough risk of something bad happening.
